📜 Introduction to Surah An-Noor (The Light)

Surah An-Noor is the 24th chapter of the Holy Quran, containing 64 verses. It was revealed in Madinah and is named after the famous "Verse of Light" (Ayat An-Noor, verse 35), which describes Allah as the Light of the Heavens and the Earth.

This Surah primarily addresses the moral and social ethics of the Muslim community, focusing on topics such as truthfulness, modesty, honour, privacy, family life, and seeking permission before entering someone's home.

Madani Surah 64 Verses Surah No. 24

🌹 The Incident of Ifk & Hazrat Ayesha (RA)

One of the most important historical contexts of Surah An-Noor is the incident of Ifk (the false accusation). This refers to the deeply painful event in which Hazrat Ayesha bint Abi Bakr (RA), the beloved wife of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, was falsely accused of wrongdoing by the hypocrites of Madinah.

Hazrat Ayesha (RA) — one of the most knowledgeable, pious and honoured women in Islamic history — had fallen behind during the return journey from the Banu Mustaliq expedition. She was then escorted back to Madinah by a Companion, and this innocent situation was twisted by the hypocrites into a slander against her honour.

The Prophet ﷺ and the believing community were deeply distressed. Hazrat Ayesha (RA) herself maintained patience and placed her trust entirely in Allah. After approximately a month of waiting, Allah revealed verses (An-Noor 11–20) directly declaring her innocence and condemning those who spread the false accusation.

These verses teach us: never spread accusations about innocent people without four witnesses; avoid and condemn slander; protect the honour of believers; and that Allah Himself is the ultimate Protector of truth and innocence.

✨ The Famous Verse of Light — Ayat An-Noor (24:35)

ٱللَّهُ نُورُ ٱلسَّمَٰوَٰتِ وَٱلۡأَرۡضِ

"Allah is the Light of the Heavens and the Earth. The example of His light is like a niche within which is a lamp; the lamp is within glass; the glass as if it were a luminous star lit from a blessed olive tree, neither of the east nor the west, whose oil would almost glow even if untouched by fire. Light upon light. Allah guides to His light whom He wills." (24:35)

This verse is considered one of the most profound metaphysical descriptions in all of scripture — describing the nature of divine guidance as multi-layered, pure, self-sustaining light that illuminates whoever Allah wills.

🏠 Social & Community Guidelines in Surah An-Noor

Beyond the incident of Ifk, Surah An-Noor established timeless community guidelines:

  • 24:27–29 Seeking permission before entering homes — protecting family privacy
  • 24:30–31 Lowering the gaze and modesty for both men and women
  • 24:11–20 Condemnation of slander and false accusation
  • 24:2 Legal standards requiring witnesses for accusations
  • 24:36–38 Houses of Allah (Mosques) and the characteristics of devoted believers

🌍 Surah An-Noor in the Modern World

The teachings of Surah An-Noor are remarkably relevant to modern challenges:

Social Media & Misinformation: Verses 11–20 warn against spreading unverified information — directly applicable to viral rumours and fake news online.

Privacy & Digital Boundaries: The principle of seeking permission (isti'dhan) translates to respecting digital privacy, personal information and boundaries.

Protecting Honour: The Surah's emphasis on protecting people's reputation is essential in an age where character assassination spreads instantly worldwide.

Modesty in All Forms: Modesty applies not just to clothing but to speech, behaviour, online content and how we represent ourselves and others.
